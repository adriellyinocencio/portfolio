About

The short version

I got into computer science because I wanted to build things that change something for someone. I like the idea that small, well-built parts can add up to something a lot bigger than any one of them — that's true of good systems, and it's the standard I hold my work to.

At UCSB I've been building my foundation with Python, C++, and algorithms. I'm still figuring out exactly where I want to specialize, but I'm drawn to human-computer interaction — how software actually feels to use — and lately I've been teaching myself Godot on the side, just because I got curious about game dev.
